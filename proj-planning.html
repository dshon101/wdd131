Plan Your Visit

Everything you need to know to make your Victoria Falls adventure smooth and memorable

When to Visit

High Water Season

February - May

  • Peak flow with maximum water volume
  • Spectacular curtain of water
  • Heavy mist can limit visibility
  • Bring rain gear for viewing
  • Most dramatic and powerful experience

Medium Water Season

June - August

  • Good balance of flow and visibility
  • Clear views of the falls
  • Pleasant weather conditions
  • Popular time for visitors
  • Ideal for photography

Low Water Season

September - December

  • Reduced water flow reveals rock face
  • Access to Devil's Pool swimming
  • Excellent for gorge views
  • Hot weather - stay hydrated
  • Unique perspective on the geology

Getting There

By Air

Harry Mwanga Nkumbula International Airport (LVI) in Livingstone is about 25km from Victoria Falls. Direct flights available from Lusaka, Johannesburg, and other regional hubs. Airport transfers can be arranged through hotels or taxi services.

By Road

Livingstone is accessible by road from Lusaka (approximately 7-8 hours) and other major Zambian cities. The roads are generally well-maintained. Car rentals available in Livingstone.

Visa Requirements

Most visitors can obtain a visa on arrival at Livingstone Airport or border crossings. A KAZA UniVisa allows entry to both Zambia and Zimbabwe. Check current requirements for your nationality before traveling.

Where to Stay

Livingstone Town: Budget-friendly hostels, mid-range hotels, and guesthouses. Good restaurants and local atmosphere. About 10km from the falls.

Near the Falls: Luxury lodges and hotels with stunning views. Higher prices but convenient location. Some offer exclusive access to viewing points.

Riverside Lodges: Upmarket accommodations along the Zambezi River. Beautiful settings with wildlife viewing and sunset cruises available.

What to Pack

Essentials

  • Passport and visa documents
  • Travel insurance information
  • Cash (USD and Zambian Kwacha)
  • Sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Water bottle

Clothing

  • Lightweight, breathable clothing
  • Waterproof jacket or poncho
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Hat and sunglasses
  • Swimsuit (for Devil's Pool)

Photography

  • Camera with waterproof protection
  • Extra batteries and memory cards
  • Dry bag for electronics
  • Lens cloth

Safety Tips

  • Stay on marked paths and respect all safety barriers
  • Listen to your guide's instructions during activities
  • Protect electronics from the constant mist near the falls
  • Stay hydrated and use sun protection
  • Be aware of wildlife in the area
  • Keep valuables secure and use hotel safes
  • Check activity age and fitness requirements before booking
