Welcome to Zimbabwe

Zimbabwe is a landlocked country in southern Africa known for its dramatic landscape and diverse wildlife. Home to the magnificent Victoria Falls, ancient stone ruins, and vibrant culture, Zimbabwe offers visitors a rich tapestry of natural wonders and historical treasures. From the thundering waters of "Mosi-oa-Tunya" to the UNESCO World Heritage sites, Zimbabwe captivates the hearts of all who visit.

Must-See Attractions

Victoria Falls

One of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World, Victoria Falls is the largest sheet of falling water on Earth. Known locally as "Mosi-oa-Tunya" (The Smoke That Thunders), it's a breathtaking spectacle of nature's power.

Great Zimbabwe Ruins

An ancient city and UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Great Zimbabwe Ruins are stone structures dating back to the 11th century. These ruins are a testament to the sophisticated kingdom that once thrived here.

Hwange National Park

Zimbabwe's largest game reserve, home to over 100 mammal species and 400 bird species. Famous for its large elephant population, it offers incredible safari experiences and wildlife viewing.

Matobo Hills

A stunning landscape of granite kopjes and wooded valleys featuring ancient rock art, diverse wildlife, and the final resting place of Cecil Rhodes. A UNESCO World Heritage Site rich in spiritual and cultural significance.

Lake Kariba

One of the world's largest man-made lakes, offering houseboat safaris, excellent fishing, and spectacular sunsets. The lake is home to hippos, crocodiles, and abundant birdlife.

Mana Pools National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site on the banks of the Zambezi River, known for its wilderness and wildlife. One of the few places in Africa where visitors can walk with elephants and experience true remote safari adventures.
